### Digital Humanities Foundations (Cite 4-5)

1. **Moretti, Franco. *Distant Reading*. Verso, 2013.**
   - **Why:** Foundational DH text; introduces "distant reading" concept
   - **Where to cite:** Introduction - framing computational approaches to literature
   - **Key quote:** "The trouble with close reading is that it necessarily depends on an extremely small canon"

2. **Ramsay, Stephen. *Reading Machines: Toward an Algorithmic Criticism*. University of Illinois Press, 2011.**
   - **Why:** Argues for algorithmic methods as critical interpretation (not just data)
   - **Where to cite:** Introduction + Reflexive Methodology section
   - **Key concept:** "Algorithmic criticism" as hermeneutic practice

3. **Underwood, Ted. *Distant Horizons: Digital Evidence and Literary Change*. University of Chicago Press, 2019.**
   - **Why:** Shows how computational methods reveal historical patterns
   - **Where to cite:** Introduction - precedent for corpus-scale pattern finding
   - **Relevant:** Chapter on genre and form

4. **Piper, Andrew. *Enumerations: Data and Literary Study*. University of Chicago Press, 2018.**
   - **Why:** Argues for "numeracy" in literary study
   - **Where to cite:** Introduction - justifying quantitative approaches
   - **Key concept:** Enumeration as critical method

5. **Drucker, Johanna. "Humanities Approaches to Graphical Display." *Digital Humanities Quarterly* 5.1 (2011).**
   - **Why:** Argues humanistic data visualization differs from scientific
   - **Where to cite:** Methodology section (when discussing visualizations)
   - **Key concept:** "Capta" vs. "data"
